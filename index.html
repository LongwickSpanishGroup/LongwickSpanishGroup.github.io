Welcome to Longwick Spanish Conversation Group

About Us

Longwick Spanish Conversation Group is a free Spanish club where people meet in informal settings and chat in Spanish.

Participation is free. However, we have a limited number of spaces available, so please don't come unannounced. Instead, please join our Facebook group, find the next event, and select 'Going'.

We meet every Tuesday at the Red Lion pub in Longwick, Buckinghamshire, from 6:30 to 8:00pm.

The pub is located at Thame Rd, Longwick, Princes Risborough HP27 9SG.

We meet in the dining area, and although there's no requirement to buy food, the pub kindly asks that each visitor supports them by purchasing at least one drink.

There's no explicit teaching involved; the idea is to try and speak about the topic we're discussion in Spanish to help unblock your conversational skills.

Our meetings are held in-person, as we do not plan to conduct any online sessions. Therefore, our group is best suited for locals residing in Longwick, Princes Risborough, Thame, Haddenham, Aylesbury, High Wycombe, Chinnor, and other nearby villages and towns.

For more information and to join us, visit our Facebook page.

If you don't have a Facebook profile but still would like to participate, please get in touch with us by email.

Spanish Conversation Group Guidelines

  1. Don't worry about making mistakes when speaking Spanish. We're all here to learn, not to judge. Remember, our mistakes are probably worse than yours!
  2. If you can't recall a word or an expression, take your time; we'll all wait. Feel free to try rephrasing or describing it. If nothing comes to mind, you can ask the group for help—similar to how you might in English if you can't recall a word. Simply say, "No puedo recordar la palabra, ¿pueden ayudarme?"
  3. Respect each other's pace and level of proficiency in Spanish. Everyone learns at their own speed, so be patient and encouraging with each other.
  4. The conversation group cannot substitute explicit learning; it's only one of the resources available to you.
  5. Share resources, such as language learning apps, books, or online articles, that have helped you improve your Spanish skills. Supporting each other outside of group meetings can enhance individual progress.
  6. Be mindful of cultural sensitivities and avoid topics that may be controversial or offensive. Respect everyone's background and perspectives.
  7. Take turns speaking and listening actively during conversations to ensure everyone has a chance to participate and practice.
  8. Have fun and enjoy the journey of learning Spanish together! Celebrate achievements, no matter how small, and encourage each other to keep progressing.
