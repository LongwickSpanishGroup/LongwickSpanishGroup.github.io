What's Available to Spanish Learners in Princes Risborough and Surrounding Areas

We're only aiming to list what's available for adult learners.

In-person Learning

  • If you're retired or semi-retired, U3A offers a monthly meeting for those who'd like to learn Spanish. It's free for members and aimed at beginners.
  • Spanish Conversation Group offers weekly meetings at the Red Lion pub in Longwick. They're free, although you're expected to buy a drink from the pub. There's no explicit teaching going on, but it's a good place to practice your spoken Spanish and socialise with like-minded people. Speakers of all levels are welcome.
  • Superprof has a list of Spanish tutors in the Princes Risborough area. The prices vary.

If you know of other resources available, please drop us a line, and we'll add them to this page.

Use Internet to Learn Spanish
These resources are available to anyone in any area

Apps to Learn Spanish

  • Duolingo. A great app/website for beginners and those who'd like to brush up their Spanish (and not only Spanish).
  • Memrise. Good for learning lists of words and expressions.
  • Assimil. Listen to conversations and then repeat them, helped by the text, the translation, the grammar notes and some exercises.
  • Some other apps available for Spanish learners that we haven't tried yet: Lingvist, Busuu, Wlingua, Drops, Fluent Forever.

Lessons and Grammar Online

  • StudySpanish. Spanish lessons and verb conjugation charts for beginners.
  • 123 TeachMe. Similar to the above. Aimed at beginners.
  • EspañolAvanzado. Lessons and articles for intermediate and advanced learners.
  • Grammar explanations and answers in Spanish for advanced learners: RAE: F.A.Q., RAE: Dictionary of Spanish usage, RAE: Complete Spanish grammar, Hispanoteca: Spanish grammar, Fundéu: Urgent questions search.

Online Translators

  • Google Translate. Free, widely available.
  • DeepL. Has a free and a paid version. Probably a tad better than Google Translate
  • ChatGPT. Strictly speaking, it's not an online translator but an AI tool that can also translate. treat ChatGPT as if you're talking to a human. For example, you can say, "Hi mate, could you please translate this text for me: '.....'. The context is an email to my co-workers in Argentina, so please use Argentinian Spanish and make sure the translation uses the formal 'usted' and is appropriate for office conversation." After you receive your translation, you can ask for clarification or request changes. Just pretend you're talking to a helpful friend. It's also free to use and has apps for Android/iOS.
